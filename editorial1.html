Travel Goals Sponsored by the Taxpayers

Written By: Ahmad Sultani In Election , Politics

If not a president, PBBM surely will become part of the tourismindustry. Within 6 months of his administration, the Philippine leader, President Ferdinand‘Bongbong’ Marcos Jr. already had 6 foreign travels which are followedby6more by the half of the year 2023. He has an average of one foreign travel per month inside these time frame. He surpassed the records of the precedingpresidents of the country regarding this matter.

It’s a waste of money just by thinking about the luxurious and extravagant hotels that Mr. Marcos will stay in for about a couple of days for his travel whilebringing his family and another company with him.

During one if his visits, 70 people accompanied him which lasteduptofive days. Just a one night stay in a hotel with these people to be accommodated, it will take about P4 million above of budget fund. This clearly models lackof stewardship towards every taxpayers’ money. With more necessary things toturnhis head onto, he chose to allocate a budget for a rich living. The leader’s senseof entitlement towards the country’s money is literally jaw-dropping.

Furthermore, Mr. Marcos also demonstrated lack of transparency towardsthe travel fund of the same trip. Because of this action, not just the citizensbut also some of his allies are doubting his goal for the Philippines. Lackof transparency also means that the leader has unclear vision and mission onthiscase scenario. Because of this, the country’s people are also being dividedwhenit comes to their opinion and stand to the current president’s administration.

Most importantly, the continuous inflation inside the country is onethingthat the government must address first before spending the nation’s money tothepresidential foreign travels. In addition, the allocated budget for theseextravagant travels could also be more useful if utilized in health field andother programs that seeks to provide the need of the majority and aims to aid themajor problems inside the country.

On one hand, these foreign travels brought partnership fromnumerouscountry that PBBM visited. Just like when he went to the United States (US) which resulted to huge investments for the country. The administration flauntsabout having the US trade mission visit the country, bringing large capitalstoinvest here. On the other hand, the expenses that Mr. Marcos spent for thesekinds of travels could almost eat up the money that the nation have. Wegainsomething but lose a significant amount.

The president’s administration must carefully take measures regardingtheplans that they have in the future. At the end of the day, it is the people’s needthat they must prioritize. The president seems to forget about this goal by lavishlyspending the taxpayers’ money to his travels. While his actions attracts foreigninvestors, the same deeds are also creating division among the peoplewhichleads to the country’s instability. If Mr. Marcos is a flight attendant, it is his veryduty to also make the passengers safety and comfortable--not designingtheplane to be extremely beautiful.
